[1m🎯 Color Palette Summary:[22m
[1m[22m
[38;5;43mThe color interface provides two complementary approaches:[39m
[38;5;43m[39m
[38;5;43m[39m
  [1mCustom Heroku colors:[22m

    • Semantic functions ([38;5;147m⬢ app[39m, [38;5;40msuccess[39m, etc.)

    • Purpose-built for Heroku CLI consistency

    • Include icons and specific styling

    • Use design system colors (147, 40, etc.)


  [1mANSIS Colors:[22m

    • Direct access to all ansis functionality

    • Standard ANSI colors ([31mred[39m, [34mblue[39m, etc.)

    • Text styling ([1mbold[22m, [3mitalic[23m, etc.)

    • Custom colors ([38;2;255;107;107mhex[39m, [38;2;255;107;107mRGB[39m)

    • Chainable methods for complex styling


[38;5;43mAll colors are designed to be accessible and consistent across the Heroku CLI experience.[39m
[38;5;43m[39m
[38;5;43mStatus colors follow semantic conventions: green for success, red for errors, orange for warnings.[39m
[38;5;43m[39m
[38;5;43mPurple is used for apps, magenta for pipelines, pink for general names, yellow for addons and datastores, and gold for attachments.[39m
[38;5;43m[39m
[38;5;43mBoth approaches can be used together for maximum flexibility while maintaining Heroku CLI design consistency.[39m
